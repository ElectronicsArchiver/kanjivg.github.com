Conventions of KanjiVG

This page details some choices made by KanjiVG. If you wish to discuss changing the individual choices, please use the mailing list at Google Groups to bring up your proposal.

Radicals used

This section details the choices made for the values of kvg:element, the visual representation of a kanji radical.

Sanzui

The radical used for sanzui elements (the variant form of Kangxi radical 85, "water") is U+6C35 氵 rather than U+2EA1 ⺡, except for the special case of U+2EA1 itself.

Kemono-hen

The radical used for kemono-hen elements (the variant form of Kangxi radical 94, "dog") is U+2EA8 ⺨ rather than U+72AD 犭, except for the special case of U+72AD itself.

Shin-nyō

The radical used for shin-nyō elements (Kangxi radical 162, "walk") is either U+8FB6 辶 or U+2ECC ⻌ depending on whether the radical consists of four or three elements respectively. Confusingly, the visual display of 8FB6 in a font may appear to contain only three strokes.

The visually highly similar U+2ECD from CJK Radicals Supplement, and U+FA66 from CJK Compatibility Ideograph are not used by KanjiVG.
